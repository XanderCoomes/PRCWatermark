Teaching Evolution (Plant Transformation) Through Dialogue with Machines

Using artificial intelligence to learn about plant transformation through dialogue interactions is a curious blend of technology and nature, one that pushes at how we think about teaching science — especially one as intricate and time-sensitive as evolution.

Imagine walking into a classroom where two student-participants sit across from each other: one sees the letters “DNA,” while the other stares blankly into a digital photograph of petals slowly changing color under new sunlight conditions. Neither speaks first. Instead, they start asking questions. The atmosphere unfolds not like a textbook lecture, but more like nature’s quiet handwork, probing edge cases
