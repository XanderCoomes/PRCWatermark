It is a common belief that artificial intelligence (AI) has transformed many sectors, including healthcare, finance, transportation, and manufacturing. However, its application in education remains relatively underexplored despite promising potential. The integration of AI into educational systems presents both opportunities and challenges, requiring careful consideration from educators, policymakers, and technologists alike.

One significant advantage of using AI in teaching lies in personalized learning experiences. Traditional classrooms often struggle to meet individual student needs due to class sizes and limited resources.
