The Use of Artificial Intelligence in Education: A Historical Perspective

Introduction  
Throughout human civilization’s long evolution, learning has served as a cornerstone for societal advancement—shaping cultures, promoting innovation, and enabling social cohesion. The foundation of education exists not only in formal classrooms but also within systems created by non-human or “artificial” means dating back millennia. Though artificial intelligence (AI)—as understood today—is still a product of modern scientific achievement—it is essential to recognize that humanity's pursuit of intellectually useful technology intersects with educators' goals long before digital computation.
