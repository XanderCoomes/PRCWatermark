The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into education has marked a significant shift, transforming how teaching and learning occur today. From intelligent tutoring systems to automated grading tools, AI is redefining pedagogical practices across diverse educational settings—from primary schools to university classrooms—delivering more personalized, efficient, and effective instructional experiences.

One of the most impactful areas where artificial intelligence contributes significantly is personalization of learning. Every student learns at their own pace and style; recognizing this diversity requires robust assessment strategies that can track not just achievement but also comprehension patterns over time.
