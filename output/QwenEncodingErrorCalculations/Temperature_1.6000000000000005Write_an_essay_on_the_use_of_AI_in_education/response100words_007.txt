The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) into educational systems has ignited a profound conversation about how we shape learning environments for future generations. At its core, this discussion is not merely technical—it raises critical ethical questions that challenge our values as educators, policy makers, and society at large. While many advocates highlight benefits such as improved personalized instruction and enhanced administrative functions, I argue that without rigorous attention to ethics, fairness, and equitable access, AI may deepen existing societal inequalities instead of resolving them.
