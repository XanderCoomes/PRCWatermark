There is a sign in my classroom that says: "Some things cannot be taught by computers." It reads in bold letters, standing out from everything else — reminding me daily not to give up teaching. In fact, every day I remind myself that human learning involves empathy, shared experience, context, relationships, nuance and intentionality.

I am not alone in this sentiment among educators who see the transformative potential but also limitations of artificial intelligence (AI) within classrooms and curriculum design.
